The operating system behind a founder-directed company

The distinction that matters

This is not the Chief AI Office. It's the operating seat.

Aloha AI runs two adjacent executive functions. They solve different problems, and you can buy either one alone.

How should we use AI?

Chief AI Office

Owns the organization's AI capability: strategy, governance, tool and model selection, agent architecture, risk, and adoption. It makes AI trustworthy and effective across the org.

How should the business run?

AI-Native COO

Owns the organization's operating performance: priorities, execution, decisions, projects, knowledge, content, revenue, and cadence — with AI built into how the work happens. It makes the business itself work.

You are here

The one-line rule: the Chief AI Office is accountable for AI as a capability; the AI-Native COO is accountable for the organization's ability to execute. Engage both when you need AI embedded throughout the company and the company redesigned around how it actually works.

The real problem

Most founders don't have an AI problem. They have an operating-system problem.

Ideas are scattered. Content disappears after it's published. The audience lives on platforms you don't own. Relationships aren't tracked. Nobody knows what's happening across the business. Work gets repeated, opportunities get missed, and the founder is the only source of institutional knowledge. AI tools get bolted on at random with no governance. We replace that with one coherent operating system.

System A

Founder & executive

Priorities, decision logs, weekly briefs, commitments, delegated decision boundaries. Removes decision fog.

System B

Knowledge & memory

Documents, conversations, research, contracts, and content made searchable, source-linked, permissioned, and reusable.

System C

Content & IP

Every idea turned into a compounding library — conversation to transcript to article to product — not just more posts.

System D

Revenue & opportunity

Partnership pipelines, sponsor tracking, lead qualification, follow-ups — so valuable relationships stop vanishing into DMs.

System E

Business operations

Projects, roles, recurring workflows, approvals, deadlines, and SOPs. AI improves visibility; humans keep accountability.

System F

AI governance

What's automated, what stays human, which data which tools may touch, what needs verification. Where this differs from a normal COO.

Two pathways

Built for the creator business and the founder-led startup

Creator Operating System

For writers, educators, researchers, podcasters, and multi-hyphenate experts whose body of work is the business.

  • Creative intelligence — ideas, research, drafts, source material
  • Publishing — production, review, scheduling, archives
  • Audience ownership — site, newsletter, first-party records
  • IP development — frameworks, courses, licensing, media
  • Commercial ops — sponsorships, speaking, products
  • Relationship intelligence — collaborators, press, guests

Startup Operating System

For founder-led, pre-seed through Series A companies with product momentum but weak internal systems.

  • Founder command center — priorities, approvals, metrics
  • Company knowledge — decisions, product, customer intelligence
  • Operating cadence — weekly execution, accountability
  • Market & competitor monitoring
  • Investor-update and board-prep workflows
  • Partnership pipeline and agent governance

Ownership and boundaries

You keep the decisions. And you keep your data.

An AI-native COO improves visibility, preparation, and execution. It does not absorb accountability, and it does not let autonomous agents take irreversible actions.

Aloha AI owns

  • Operating architecture and system design
  • Executive preparation and workflow orchestration
  • Cross-functional visibility and agent management
  • Documentation, recommendations, and escalation

You own

  • Final business, financial, and employment decisions
  • Legal approvals and regulated professional judgment
  • Public representations and brand approvals
  • Your information — exportable, portable, yours

Not included by default: acting as legal counsel, bookkeeping or tax filing, 24/7 availability, guaranteeing revenue, publishing unapproved material, or autonomous irreversible actions. RN Collins is a JD candidate, not a licensed attorney.
