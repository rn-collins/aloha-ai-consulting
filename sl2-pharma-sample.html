Sample Brief
Standard AI tools hallucinate on exactly the facts that matter most in drug development: wrong APQ figures, wrong statutory triggers, wrong CFR citations, wrong case names. The Regulatory Intelligence Layer (RIL) is a primary-source data methodology built to close that gap — statutes, administrative records, docket filings, APQ data, CPT coding records, case law — organized to answer the specific questions operators need answered before making commercial or capital decisions.

The Operator Execution Gap — where AI breaks in your domain

OEG — Defined
"The specific analytical deficiency between what an operator needs to know and what current AI tools can reliably produce in the regulatory domain."
Example: Ask any major LLM for the current DEA Annual Production Quota for psilocybin research, the status of a § 811(j) interim final rule, or whether a specific molecule's IND triggers REMS requirements. You will get confident, plausible, and wrong answers. The RIL exists because the wrong answer at that stage of development is not a minor inconvenience — it is a capital allocation error.
