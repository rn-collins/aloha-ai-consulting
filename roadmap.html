Where this goes next

Expansion vectors

The Regulatory, Scientific, and Legal Intelligence Layer methodology is not limited to psychedelics. Nine expansion vectors extend the same analytical architecture into adjacent regulated domains — applying the same primary-source discipline across DEA scheduling, FDA pathways, scientific literature, and comparative legal analysis. Each vector is a new service line that does not require starting over. The methodology, tool stack, and analytical discipline are identical. The domain changes. The intelligence deepens.

Tier 1 — Record expansion
V1 — DEA Controlled Substances Beyond Psychedelics

The same scheduling methodology applied across the full DEA controlled substance schedule — stimulants, opioids, benzodiazepines, cannabinoids. The infrastructure already exists.

V2 — Rare Disease and Orphan Drug Programs

Orphan drug designation, breakthrough therapy, fast track, accelerated approval — the incentive and exclusivity architecture for rare disease programs.

V3 — Combination Products Beyond Psychedelics

FDA/DEA classification, psychological support vs. psychotherapy distinction, the § 813 analogue analysis — extended to combination products in other therapeutic areas.

Tier 2 — Sub-topic extension
V4 — Accelerated Approval and FDORA

The confirmatory trial regime under the FDA Omnibus Reform Act, including post-market requirements, withdrawal procedures, and real-world evidence standards.

V5 — 505(b)(2) Hybrid Pathway Programs

The hybrid approval pathway that bridges new and existing drug data — reference listed drug reliance, patent certification, Paragraph IV litigation strategy.

V6 — AI/ML Software as Medical Device

FDA SaMD classification, the Digital Health Center of Excellence framework, predetermined change control plans, and digital mental health regulatory positioning.

Tier 3 — New module builds
V7 — Gene Therapy and CAR-T Cell Therapy

The complex regulatory architecture for advanced therapy medicinal products — manufacturing oversight, long-term follow-up requirements, REMS design, and coverage pathway.

V8 — Biosimilars and the BPCIA Patent Dance

Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act procedure, the information exchange sequence, interchangeability designation, and market exclusivity layering.

V9 — Radiopharmaceuticals with NRC Integration

The dual FDA/NRC regulatory jurisdiction over radiopharmaceuticals — IND/NDA pathway alongside radioactive materials licensing, handling requirements, and reimbursement architecture.

Tier 3 vectors represent original research architecture — no published template exists for these analytical instruments. Each requires the same methodology across all three layer types: regulatory records, scientific literature, and legal/policy analysis — primary-source only, built by hand, made queryable, maintained over time. The strategic question each vector answers: is the intelligence layer a feature of a client platform, or a licensed data product for which any single platform is the anchor customer?
