One-Page Brief
Legal AI platforms face a compliance landscape that is moving faster than any individual team can track: ABA Model Rules on AI, state bar guidance, EU AI Act obligations for high-risk systems, FTC AI claims enforcement, and emerging judicial AI disclosure requirements. The Policy/Legal Intelligence Layer is a primary-source monitoring and analysis system built specifically for platforms where compliance failure is a professional liability exposure, not just a reputational one.

The questions legal AI platforms cannot currently answer reliably

Which state bars have issued formal AI guidance that affects your platform's use by licensed attorneys?
As of 2025–2026, 30+ states have issued AI-specific ethics guidance or proposed rule amendments — and the number is growing monthly. General AI tools cannot give you an accurate, current, jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction answer.
Does your platform's output trigger EU AI Act obligations as a high-risk AI system in the legal domain?
The Act's Article 6 classification criteria for legal-domain AI are not settled, and guidance is evolving. A wrong answer here is a market-access decision, not just a compliance note.
What judicial AI disclosure requirements currently apply to attorneys using your platform in federal litigation?
District-level standing orders on AI disclosure are proliferating without central coordination. No single published source tracks them accurately.
How does FTC AI claims enforcement — and ABA Formal Opinion 512 — apply to your platform's marketing and representations to attorney users?
The FTC's AI enforcement posture and ABA Formal Opinion 512 (2024) together create specific obligations for accuracy representations in legal AI platforms. Standard AI tools get the jurisdiction-specific details wrong.
