Why this, why now

Most AI adoption fails quietly, and no one can say why

Businesses are spending on AI tools with unclear ROI and no plan. Pilots stall, teams pick the flashy use case over the valuable one, and the risky workflow gets automated first. When leadership asks whether it worked, nobody has an answer they can defend.

What is missing is a rigorous audit: a repeatable way to map the real workflows, score which ones are ready, flag which ones are risky, and rank what to do first. Build that audit once and you own a method you can sell to every business that is guessing.

Curriculum

Nine modules, every one ships part of the real system

You do not watch and forget. You leave each module with a working piece of your audit method and tool.

  1. The problem and the standard. Why AI adoption fails, what AI-readiness actually means, and what a defensible audit has to prove. You write your audit rubric.
  2. Set up your workspace. Accounts, model access, project structure, with zero assumptions and nothing technical taken for granted.
  3. Map the workflows. Build the intake step that captures how a business really operates, from a short interview or existing docs.
  4. Score AI-readiness. Build the scoring engine: data quality, repeatability, volume, and fit, applied consistently across workflows.
  5. Classify the risk, the core. Flag where an AI error is costly, regulated, or irreversible. This is what makes your roadmap defensible.
  6. Prioritize and roadmap. Combine readiness and risk into a ranked plan: quick wins now, strategic bets sequenced.
  7. Governance layer. Traceable scores, uncertainty handling, default-to-human, and an audit log of every assessment.
  8. Evaluate your audit. Run it on two real businesses; check the roadmap holds up; tune the rubric where it does not.
  9. Sell the audit. Package it as a paid engagement: scoping, pricing, deliverables, and the done-with-you and done-for-you ladder.

A client's highest-volume workflow scored high on readiness. Should that be the first quick win?
