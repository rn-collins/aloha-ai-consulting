      5. Adoption — where 2026 pilots actually fail

      The technology rarely fails; adoption does. Trust is a neurological state: repeated safe exposure lowers threat response and builds the habit loop. Set an estimated adoption rate per stage to see where your funnel leaks.

      Three change-management levers

      • Safe exposure: low-stakes, reversible first tasks so the threat response never fires. Trust is built by repetition, not by a training deck.
      • Visible governance: when lawyers can see the verification gate and audit trail, the tool feels like a safeguard rather than a liability — converting exposure into trust.
      • Peer advocacy: a respected internal champion who demonstrates wins moves habitual users to advocates faster than any top-down mandate.

      Where 2026 pilots actually fail: the drop-off between trust and habitual use, not the model quality.
