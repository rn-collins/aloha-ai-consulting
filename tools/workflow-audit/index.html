Why this exists

A working AI habit and a mature AI workflow are two different things

Plenty of teams get good output from AI. Far fewer can say where the facts came from, who checked them, what data went into which tool, and whether the next person could run the same process and get the same quality. That gap is where risk lives. This audit checks the parts of an AI workflow that make it repeatable, verifiable, and defensible, so a good result stops depending on who happened to run it.

What it checks

Whether your workflow grounds output in real sources, gates it behind human review, protects confidential data, runs the same way every time, has documented ownership, and gets measured so it improves.

What it can't do

It cannot tell you whether your organization is compliant, and it does not give legal advice. It maps your answers to workflow-maturity practice and shows where the process is thin. A qualified advisor interprets what that means for your obligations.

What it measures

Six dimensions of AI-workflow maturity

Each question belongs to a dimension. Yes scores full credit, Partial scores half, No scores nothing. The dimensions roll into per-dimension sub-scores and one overall 0–100 maturity score, weighted evenly across the six.

01

Sources & grounding

Whether AI output is tied to real, checkable sources rather than the model's unaided guess.

02

Verification & QA

Whether a human reviews output and checks for hallucination before anything client-facing goes out.

03

Privacy & confidentiality

Whether there are rules about what data goes into which tools, and whether they are followed.

04

Handoff & reproducibility

Whether the workflow is a documented, repeatable process or an ad-hoc effort that lives in one head.

05

Governance & policy

Whether AI use is documented, owned, and accountable rather than assumed.

06

Measurement & improvement

Whether you track how the workflow performs and use what you learn to improve it.
