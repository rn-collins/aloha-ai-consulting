Why this exists

Triage is the slow, repetitive part of tracking legislation

A compliance or policy team watching a legislature reads dozens of bills to find the handful that create real obligations. The signal sits in predictable places: mandatory "shall" and "must" clauses, prohibitions, filing deadlines, dollar thresholds, and rulemaking hooks. This tool does the mechanical first pass so a human starts from a structured map instead of a wall of text. It marks where the obligations are; it does not decide what they mean.

What it surfaces

Issue tags from keyword lexicons, the stakeholders a text implicates, obligation language quoted in context, deadlines and dollar thresholds, and a risk read that reflects how much of that language is present.

What it will not do

It does not read for meaning, weigh legislative intent, resolve ambiguity, or tell you whether an obligation applies to your client. Pattern matching finds candidate language. A licensed attorney reads the source and decides. The questions-for-counsel list is built for exactly that handoff.

      How it works

      Pattern matching, in a fixed order

      Every run applies the same deterministic passes to the same text, so the same input always returns the same map. No model, no randomness, no network.

      Tag the issues

      The text is scanned against keyword lexicons for privacy and data, AI and automated decisions, licensing, penalties and enforcement, fees and taxes, reporting and disclosure, definitions, effective dates, rulemaking delegation, and preemption.

      Infer the stakeholders

      Terms like operator, licensee, consumer, agency, employer, and provider map to the parties a text reaches, so you see at a glance who carries the obligations.

      Pull the triggers

      Mandatory language ("shall", "must", "required to", "no person shall", "prohibited"), calendar deadlines, and dollar thresholds are quoted with the surrounding sentence, so nothing is paraphrased.

      Read risk, summarize, and hand off

      Obligation density rolls into a High, Medium, or Low read. A plain-English summary is assembled from section counts, defined terms, and any effective date. A questions-for-counsel checklist frames the handoff to a licensed attorney.
