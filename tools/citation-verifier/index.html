Why this exists

A hallucinated case name is a filing you can't take back

Since 2023, courts have sanctioned lawyers for filing AI-fabricated citations. Generic AI hallucinates worst on exactly what a legal team can't get wrong: the citation, the reporter, the year. Under ABA Formal Opinion 512, the duty to verify AI-assisted output before it reaches a court sits with a licensed attorney and cannot be handed off to a tool. This instrument is the first gate: it catches the cites that are structurally impossible before a human even reads them, so the review that follows starts from a shorter, sharper list.

What it flags

Invented reporter series (there is no F.4d or F. Supp. 5th), years that can't exist (a published opinion dated in the future), and malformed volumes or pages. These are the fingerprints a fabricated cite usually leaves.

What it can't do alone

A well-formed citation can still point to no real opinion. The offline engine confirms structure only. Confirming that a plausible cite matches a real case takes the live existence check, and, in every case, a licensed attorney who opens the source.

    How it works

    Deterministic checks now, live existence check next

    Two layers, in order. The fast structural layer runs on your device and needs no network. The live layer adds the one signal structure can't give: whether a plausible-looking cite matches a real published opinion.

    Parse every citation

    The engine reads US reporter citations out of the draft: volume, reporter, page, and year, plus the case name where it can find one. This runs entirely in your browser.

    Check the structure

    Each cite is tested against a known-reporter allow-list and series ceilings, so an invented series like F.4d or F. Supp. 5th is caught, along with impossible years and malformed volumes or pages. A defect this clear marks a likely fabrication.

    Score and gate

    Verdicts roll into a 0–100 risk score. A pre-filing checklist stays blocked until a licensed attorney certifies each item, because the ABA-512 duty to verify is non-delegable.

    Confirm existence, live

    The optional live check queries CourtListener for whether each well-formed cite matches a real opinion. In a browser this is a personal demo; a firm deployment routes it through a private backend so the token never sits client-side and CORS never blocks it.
