The Ownership Gap · governance vs. vibes

"Just train the team on ChatGPT." Watch that answer collapse.

The default reflex is to buy licenses and run a lunch-and-learn. That treats AI as a tooling problem. It's an ownership problem. Same situation, two responses: the generic training-first reflex, and the governance-grounded read that a Fractional CAO actually delivers. The distance between them is where the risk lives.

✕ Generic reflex · tool-first

"We should take AI seriously — let's train everyone on ChatGPT and see what sticks."

"Roll out licenses, run a training, encourage experimentation. Adoption will follow and we'll figure out policy later."

Feels like progress. Leaves four holes open — no one owns adoption, no one owns risk, there's no policy, and vendor/data governance is nobody's job. "Later" is when the incident happens.

Training is one lever of four, and not the first. Before adoption, someone has to own the decision: set the strategy, name accountability, and stand up the guardrails. How it works

Assess → Govern → Run: a repeatable strategy method

A Fractional CAO engagement isn't a slide deck. It's the same three-stage pipeline every time, adapted from regulatory monitoring to strategy: read the ground truth, resolve it into governance, then run it.

Assess: Readiness Intelligence Layer (RIL)

Ground truth first: current AI use, shadow tools, data flows, existing policy (or its absence), and where accountability actually sits. Scored against NIST AI RMF, the EU AI Act, and ISO/IEC 42001, not against a vendor's checklist.

Govern: Resolve to an Ownership Layer (ROL)

The assessment becomes decisions leadership can act on: a written strategy, an AI policy, a named oversight structure, vendor and data-handling guardrails, and a prioritized adoption plan. The gaps close in an order that matches your risk, not alphabetically.

Run: ongoing fractional ownership

Someone holds the mandate on a recurring cadence: driving adoption, keeping governance current as tools and rules shift, and answering to the board. From 2 days/week to full ownership, so it never goes leaderless again.
