Why this exists

The most common mistake is building too much

Teams hear "AI" and picture a chatbot trained on their files. Often the real problem is that nobody can find last quarter's contract, and a naming convention plus a shared drive would fix it for a weekend of effort. The wrong system costs money, stalls, and gets abandoned. This checker walks down a ladder from the cheapest fix to the most involved one and stops at the first rung that meets your actual need.

What it does

Takes your answers about volume, formats, change rate, access, sensitivity, and what you need to do with the documents, then points to one primary recommendation with runner-ups, a plain-English rationale, and a first-week checklist.

What it will not do

It will not sell you the expensive option by default, and it does not give legal, security, or financial advice. It maps your inputs to a sensible starting point. A short conversation confirms whether that starting point is right for your specifics.

    The six options

    A ladder from cheapest fix to most involved

    The checker weighs your answers and stops at the first rung that meets your real need. Higher rungs are more capable and more costly to build and maintain. The goal is the lowest rung that solves your problem, not the highest one you could imagine.

    Rung A

    Organize folders + naming

    A clean folder structure and a naming convention on the drive you already pay for. Cheapest, fastest, and enough for most small, stable document sets.

    Rung B

    Write SOPs first

    When the pain is a repeatable process rather than the files, a few written procedures beat any tool. Often the right first move before you build anything.

    Rung C

    Notion or a doc hub

    A shared workspace with pages, light structure, and search. Good when a handful of people need one place for living documents and notes.

    Rung D

    Airtable or a database

    When your records are consistent fields (clients, cases, inventory), a structured database beats documents. Filter, sort, and relate instead of hunting.

    Rung E

    RAG / AI retrieval

    When you need to ask questions across a large, changing pile of documents, AI retrieval reads them and answers with citations. Powerful, and the most setup.

    Rung F

    Custom application

    A purpose-built app when off-the-shelf tools truly cannot fit the workflow. The most capable and the most expensive; earn your way here.
