Important limits on this tool

Read this first. RN Collins is a JD candidate, not a licensed attorney. This generator produces a template for review by your firm's licensed attorneys. It is not legal advice, not a compliance determination, and it creates no reliance and no attorney–client relationship. The output is a starting draft mapped to published ABA Formal Opinion 512 and Model-Rule guidance, to be reviewed and adapted by counsel admitted in your jurisdiction. State rules vary.

Why this exists

ABA 512 made "govern your AI" a written obligation

ABA Formal Opinion 512 reads the existing Model Rules onto generative AI: competent, confidential, candid, supervised use already applies. A firm that uses AI well but writes none of it down cannot show the practice when it counts. This generator gives you a first draft of that written record, with your firm's own tools, roles, and retention preferences interpolated into the clauses each duty implies. The Governance Readiness Scorecard tells you where your documented practice is thin; this tool gives you a document to start closing that gap.

What it produces

A structured policy: purpose and scope, approved tools, roles and sign-off, a duty-by-duty mapping to the Model Rules, a mandatory verification workflow, audit and retention terms, prohibited uses, and acknowledgment. Your inputs land in the correct clauses.

What it can't do

It cannot adapt to your jurisdiction's specific rules, and it does not give legal advice. It maps to published ABA and Model-Rule guidance and hands you a draft. A licensed attorney reviews and adapts it before adoption.

What it covers

Eight sections, mapped to the duties

The draft mirrors a working firm policy: your inputs write into scope, tools, roles, retention, and the duty-by-duty controls. Each rule maps to a concrete step, not an abstract principle.

Rule 1.1

Competence

The lawyer must understand what the tool can and cannot do, including that it can fabricate authority. The draft ties this to a verification control.

Rule 1.6

Confidentiality

Client information only enters approved, vetted tools. Your confidentiality preference shapes this clause.

Rule 3.3

Candor to the tribunal

A non-delegable pre-filing gate: no AI-assisted filing until a responsible attorney certifies every authority.

Rules 5.1 / 5.3

Supervision

Your sign-off role reviews the audit record and enforces the policy over lawyers and non-lawyer assistance alike.

Workflow

Verification & audit

A mandatory verification workflow and an audit trail retained for the period you set, so the practice can be shown.

Governance

Prohibited uses & acknowledgment

Clear prohibitions and an annual acknowledgment requirement for everyone who touches AI on client work.
