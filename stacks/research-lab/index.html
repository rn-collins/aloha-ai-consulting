Layer by layer

What each layer does, and where AI breaks

For every layer: the job, where AI earns real hours, where it fails in ways a reviewer will catch, and the tools and services that cover it.

Layer 01

Literature review

The job. Find the relevant work, understand the state of the field, and not miss the one paper that undercuts your hypothesis.

Where AI helps. Summarizing papers, surfacing related work, extracting methods and findings, and drafting a review skeleton a researcher checks against the originals.

Where it fails. General models invent plausible-looking citations, misstate what a study found, and cite retracted or non-existent papers with full confidence. Every reference has to be verified against the source.

Maps tothe Regulatory & Market Intelligence service for sourced, dated evidence streams, and Knowledge Base Readiness Checker to decide how to store what you gather before you overbuild a system.

Layer 02

Evidence matrices

The job. Turn a pile of papers into a structured comparison: study, design, sample, outcome, limitation, so patterns and gaps become visible.

Where AI helps. Extracting fields into a consistent table, flagging inconsistencies across studies, and drafting the first version of a synthesis grid.

Where it fails. Confident mis-extraction: a model records the wrong effect size or sample and the error propagates through the whole matrix. Extraction needs a verification pass, not blind trust.

Maps toKnowledge Base Readiness Checker to size the structure the matrix should live in, from a well-built sheet up to a searchable base, with the Regulatory & Market Intelligence service where live sources feed it.

Layer 03

Note systems

The job. Keep reading notes, ideas, and connections findable over months, so the lab builds knowledge instead of re-reading the same papers.

Where AI helps. Retrieval over your own notes, linking related ideas, and answering internal questions with a pointer back to the source note.

Where it fails. Overbuilding is the trap: standing up a heavy RAG system when a disciplined note structure would serve, or letting the model answer from general training instead of your actual notes.

Maps toKnowledge Base Readiness Checker to get a straight answer on folders vs. a base vs. a custom app, plus Content & Adoption Systems to build note-to-draft habits the team keeps.

Layer 04

Publication support

The job. Move faster from findings to a submittable manuscript: structure, prose, formatting, and response-to-reviewer drafts.

Where AI helps. First-draft sections from an outline, tightening dense passages, reformatting for a target journal, and drafting reviewer responses a PI edits.

Where it fails. Invented references and subtly wrong restatements of your own results. AI drafts what sounds right, and in a methods or results section that is exactly the wrong place to trust it.

Maps toCitation Verifier to screen any references a model produces, and Content & Adoption Systems to build a research-to-publication pipeline the lab reuses.

Layer 05

Policy translation

The job. Turn a finding into a brief, a comment letter, or an explainer that a policymaker or funder actually reads and trusts.

Where AI helps. Reformatting a paper into plain language, drafting a policy one-pager, and adjusting register for a non-specialist audience.

Where it fails. Claims that drift past what the evidence supports, and citations dropped in translation. The policy version has to carry the same caveats the paper did.

Maps tothe Regulatory & Market Intelligence service to keep translated claims sourced and dated, and Regulatory Bill Analyzer where the work touches a specific bill or rule.
