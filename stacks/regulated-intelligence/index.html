Layer by layer

What each layer does, and where AI breaks

For every layer: the job, where AI earns real time, where it fails in fast-moving markets, and the live monitors and tools that cover it. External monitors open in a new tab.

Layer 01

Scheduling tracking

The job. Watch DEA and Federal Register actions on cannabinoids and psychedelics, and model your exposure to a scheduling change before it lands.

Where AI helps. Summarizing a Federal Register notice, flagging what changed, and drafting a plain-language read of a scheduling action an expert verifies.

Where it fails. General models work from stale training and will describe an old schedule as current, or miss a notice entirely. This layer has to track postings as they publish, not recall them.

Maps toAloha DEA Tracker for scheduling actions as they post, and the Cannabis Rescheduling Monitor to model Schedule III exposure with every figure sourced and dated.

Layer 02

State-by-state status

The job. Know where psilocybin, MDMA, ketamine, and cannabis actually stand in each state and agency, because the map is different in every jurisdiction.

Where AI helps. Clustering status across states, drafting a jurisdiction summary, and flagging which agencies are mid-rulemaking.

Where it fails. A model will blur state lines, apply one state's rule to another, and state a status that a rule changed last month. Status has to come from a live sweep of the agencies writing the rules.

Maps toPsych Ops Intel for a psilocybin sweep across OHA, DORA, NMDOH and the other agencies, and the Psychedelic Reg Radar for status by state and federal posture.

Layer 03

Product-claim substantiation

The job. Keep marketing and label claims inside what a regulator will accept, in a market where a therapeutic claim draws enforcement fast.

Where AI helps. Scanning copy for high-risk medical and therapeutic language, flagging claims by regulator zone, and drafting safer alternatives for review.

Where it fails. A model will approve a claim that FTC or a state regulator would not, or invent a substantiation standard. This layer flags risk; a lawyer still signs off.

Maps toRegulated Claims Checker, tuned for cannabis, psychedelics, and wellness, flagging medical, therapeutic, substantiation, and consumer-protection claims by risk level.

Layer 04

Platform-suppression defense

The job. Document and respond when platforms throttle psychoactive or harm-reduction content, so reach loss is evidence, not a mystery.

Where AI helps. Logging incidents in a consistent format, spotting patterns across platforms, and drafting an appeal from a documented record.

Where it fails. A model cannot tell you why reach dropped without data, and will guess. This layer needs a real incident log, not a plausible story.

Maps tothe Aloha Suppression Sweep with 50+ documented incidents and a rolling log, plus the Regulatory & Market Intelligence service to build suppression defense into your content operation.
