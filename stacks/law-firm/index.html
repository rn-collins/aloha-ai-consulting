The Law Firm AI Stack, built so speed never becomes a sanctions risk.

A firm does not need one AI tool. It needs a stack: a layer that finds the law, a layer that holds the firm's own knowledge, a layer that drafts, a layer that verifies every cite before it leaves the building, and a governance layer that documents the whole thing. Below is what that stack looks like layer by layer, where AI earns its keep, where it still hallucinates on the exact thing that matters, and which of my live tools and services cover each layer.

Layer by layer

What each layer does, and where AI breaks

For every layer: the job it does, where AI earns real time, where it fails in ways that matter to a firm, and the real assets that cover it. The tools are live and free to run; the service is the version I build and hand over.

Layer 01

Legal research

The job. Find controlling authority, read the line of cases, and know what the current rule is before an associate spends six hours on it.

Where AI helps. Fast first passes: framing an issue, surfacing search terms, summarizing a long opinion, drafting a research memo skeleton an attorney then checks.

Where it fails. General models invent citations that look perfect and do not exist, misstate holdings, and cite overruled cases with total confidence. The failure lands exactly where the stakes are highest.

Maps toCitation Verifier to screen every cite a model returns, backed by the Legal AI Workflow service that gates research before it reaches a draft.

Layer 02

Internal knowledge base

The job. Make the firm's own briefs, precedents, clause libraries, and matter history findable, so the fourth associate to face an issue does not start from zero.

Where AI helps. Retrieval over your own documents, drafting from your own prior work, and answering internal questions with citations back to the source file.

Where it fails. Many firms overbuild this, standing up a RAG system when a well-structured shared drive would do, or underbuild it and let the model answer from general training instead of firm knowledge.

Maps toKnowledge Base Readiness Checker to size the build before you commit, then the Legal AI Workflow service to wire retrieval to firm sources with a citation trail.

Layer 03

Client-alert workflow

The job. Catch a regulatory or case-law change, decide which clients it touches, and get an accurate alert out before the client reads it somewhere else.

Where AI helps. Drafting the alert from a source document, tailoring tone per audience, and clustering which practice groups and clients a change affects.

Where it fails. A model will summarize a rule change with a subtle error in the effective date or the trigger, and a client alert with a wrong deadline is worse than no alert. It also invents urgency that is not there.

Maps toAI Workflow Audit to score where the alert pipeline leaks, plus Regulatory Bill Analyzer to read a bill into issue tags and compliance triggers before anyone drafts.

Layer 04

Document review & drafting support

The job. Move faster on first drafts, review sets, and markups: contracts, briefs, discovery, and the long tail of documents that eat associate hours.

Where AI helps. First-draft clauses, issue-spotting a review set, comparing versions, and turning a bullet outline into prose an attorney refines.

Where it fails. Confident wrong summaries of what a document says, invented facts in a draft, and privilege or confidentiality exposure when work product runs through a consumer tool with no data controls.

Maps toTrust-Safe console for a governed drafting surface with verification built in, delivered through the Legal AI Workflow service on whatever platform the firm already runs.

Layer 05

Citation verification & governance

The job. Nothing leaves the firm with a cite no one checked, and the firm can show, on paper, that AI use met the duties of competence, confidentiality, candor, and supervision.

Where AI helps. Structural checks on citations at speed, flagging malformed or impossible cites, and drafting the governance policy itself from the firm's actual workflow.

Where it fails. This is the layer you do not delegate to the model alone. Verification and governance are where a human sign-off and a documented record have to sit, because this is what a court and a bar reviewer look at.

Maps toCitation Verifier as the gate, Governance Readiness Scorecard for ABA Formal Opinion 512 posture, and the Trust-Safe console for a signed audit record.

Layer 06

Intake & operations

The job. Run the practice around the AI: intake triage, conflicts, matter setup, time capture, and the handoffs that decide whether the stack above gets used at all.

Where AI helps. Drafting intake summaries, routing matters, and cutting the administrative friction that keeps attorneys from adopting the rest of the stack.

Where it fails. Adoption is a human problem. A stack no one trusts sits unused, and a rollout that skips training and habit design stalls no matter how good the tools are. That is the neuroscience side of the work.

Maps toAI Workflow Audit across sources, verification, privacy, handoff, governance, and measurement, delivered through the Legal AI Workflow service with adoption designed in.
