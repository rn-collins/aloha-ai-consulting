What this page is

Operator receipts, sorted by what they do

Most portfolios list mockups. This one lists deployments, and it is honest about what each one is. Every build was made under a real constraint: a filing that cannot cite a fake case, a regulatory posture that shifts weekly, a knowledge base a clinician or a lawyer has to trust. Read the wall in three tiers, strongest first.

Working products

Software that does the job end to end. The flagship is the Citation Verifier: a real engine with a test suite that flags fake, impossible, and malformed case cites. It flags for human review; it does not replace attorney verification.

Demonstrators & dated references

Interactive tools and curated regulatory dashboards, each dated to the day it was captured. Real, deterministic logic and honest scope: screening and reference, not live oracles. The maintained, auto-updating versions are the paid build.

Advisory frameworks

Named methods, risk-tiering, human-review design, and domain-specific evaluation, that ship as the diagnostic layer inside an engagement. Intellectual property, not software marketed as a platform.

Selected work

legal AI Live

Trust-Safe AI Operating Layer

Context
Legal teams run AI across research, drafting, and retrieval with no shared layer to govern it, and a fabricated or impossible citation can reach a filing.
What was built
A five-tab console for orchestration, citation verification, governance, evaluation, and adoption, backed by a working verifier engine that flags fake and impossible cites and generates an ABA-512 policy. Built firm-agnostic, so it drops into any practice.
What changed
Verification sits inside the workflow as a gate, so a wrong citation gets caught before it becomes a court's problem, with an audit trail behind each check.

legal AI Live

Citation Verifier

Context
AI-assisted legal drafting can surface a citation that reads clean but points at a case that does not exist or a reporter volume that could not hold it, and a fabricated cite in a filing is an expensive mistake.
What was built
A free on-site diagnostic that checks a citation, flags fake and structurally impossible cites, shows each check it ran, and states plainly what it can and cannot conclude. It is the same verifier logic that sits inside the Trust-Safe console, offered on its own.
What changed
A drafter can screen a citation before it reaches a filing, with the reasoning visible rather than a single pass-or-fail verdict.
Open the free tools
law careers Live

FlexJD SBA Resource Hub

Context
Northeastern Law's remote FlexJD cohort learns at a distance, so the informal signals a full-time student picks up on campus, which fellowships are open, which observance the school is marking this month, what a first-year needs to know, reach the remote students last or not at all.
What was built
A student-maintained resource hub for the cohort, carrying a newsletter, an opportunity tracker, a student guide, and monthly awareness campaigns, each tied to the law behind the observance it marks. Built by RN as a student for her own cohort, not a client engagement.
What changed
Remote FlexJD students get one standing place for the opportunities and orientation that used to move by word of mouth, kept current on a schedule instead of surfacing by luck.
Open the hub
AI governance Live

Trust-Safe Twins

Context
Teams want an AI twin that can speak for a person or a brand, but a twin with no controls can answer where it was never authorized and pass for human, which is the exact failure a regulated operator cannot carry.
What was built
A governed AI-twin product line with a working sales-twin prototype, wired with consent, provenance, and disclosure controls, so a twin speaks only where it is authorized and marks plainly what it is.
What changed
The governance sits inside the product rather than in a policy document beside it, so a twin ships with its consent scope and its disclosure already enforced instead of promised.
Open the twins hub

Transform Observatory

Context
Drug-policy reform moves across many jurisdictions at once, and the people tracking it have no scored way to tell a real shift from noise or to compare one reform against another on the same terms.
What was built
A market-intelligence dashboard in the Regulatory Intelligence suite, on the same shared dashboard template and scoring reform signals against the Steve Rolles reform frameworks so each move lands on a shared scale.
What changed
Reform activity becomes a dated, scored read a policy or operator team can act on, rather than a scatter of headlines with no common measure.
