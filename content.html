The method

Built on how people decide to trust, and act on, what they read

Most content advice is style tips. The EDSA architecture starts a layer earlier, at the neuroscience of trust: the signals a reader's brain checks before it decides your content is credible, and the platform signals an algorithm checks before it decides your content is safe to distribute. Design for both and the same content earns trust and keeps its reach.

Trust, by mechanism

Content structured around the cues that drive credibility and recall, so readers act on it rather than scroll past.

Evidence-grounded

Claims tied to primary sources and kept ghost-proof, which matters most in health, legal, and regulated markets where a wrong claim is costly.

Reach, defended

Compliance and distribution treated as one problem, so being careful does not mean being invisible.
