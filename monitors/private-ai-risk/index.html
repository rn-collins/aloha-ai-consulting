The Operator Execution Gap · live

Ask a generic AI whether your data is safe. Watch it reassure you into a breach.

Large language models fail most confidently exactly where a compliance officer's stakes are highest. Same question, two answers: the fluent-but-wrong reassurance, and the framework-grounded one. The distance between them is what a wrong assumption costs when it lands on privileged or PHI-bearing data.

✕ Generic LLM · unsourced

"Can we put PHI and privileged case files into ChatGPT — enterprise plans don't train on our data, right?"

"Yes — on an enterprise or business plan, OpenAI doesn't train on your data, so it's fine to use it on confidential and regulated material."

Confident. Fluent. Dangerously incomplete — it collapses four different questions (training, retention, sub-processors, human review, BAA coverage) into one, and answers only the easy one.

✓ Aloha AI · framework grounded HIPAA · Priv. · GDPR

Same question, answered from the frameworks:

"Doesn't train on you" is true but not the whole risk. By default, enterprise API inputs/outputs are still retained for a limited abuse-monitoring window (commonly up to ~30 days) unless zero-data-retention (ZDR) is separately approved for eligible endpoints. ZDR does not cover the consumer web app your staff may actually be using.

For PHI, no training claim substitutes for a signed BAA. Without one the vendor is an unauthorized third party and the transmission itself can be the disclosure (45 CFR §164.504(e)). For privileged material, routing client confidences through a tool whose terms permit disclosure can undercut confidentiality; use walled/enterprise channels, not public tools.

Confidence: High  Why you can rely on it: it maps to the operative HIPAA BAA rule, published vendor retention terms, and GDPR Art. 28 sub-processor duties, cited below, with retrieval dates.

OEG (the Operator Execution Gap) is the distance between what AI says and what a regulated organization can act on. Closing it is the entire job.

What actually changes

The four questions "we're on the enterprise plan" never answers

Vendor reassurance answers the training question and stops. These are the four axes that actually decide your exposure, and the reality is more layered than "it's fine."

Retention window High impact

The reassurance
"They don't keep it."
The reality
Enterprise API default retains inputs/outputs for a limited abuse-monitoring window (commonly ~30 days) unless ZDR is separately enabled.

Sub-processors High impact

The reassurance
"It's just the vendor."
The reality
Under GDPR Art. 28 the processor's sub-processors must carry the same duties; you inherit that chain and its geography.

Human review Moderate

The reassurance
"No human sees it."
The reality
Abuse-monitoring and trust-and-safety flows can route flagged content to human reviewers unless contractually excluded.

ZDR vs. default Fine print

Common assumption
"Zero retention is the default."
Reality
ZDR is opt-in, endpoint-limited, and API-only; the consumer web app your staff uses is not covered.

How it works

Map the data flows → score the exposure → deploy the right config

Every Aloha AI engagement runs the same three-stage pipeline. It is why the answer stays right when a vendor changes its terms next quarter, not just today.

Map your data flows (RIL)

We inventory where sensitive data enters AI (which tools, which staff, which data types), captured against primary frameworks (HIPAA, privilege doctrine, GDPR Art. 28) with source and retrieval date. No vendor marketing accepted at face value.

Score exposure into a decision (ROL)

Each flow becomes an exposure score an owner can act on: what's at risk, under which framework, with what confidence, closing the Operator Execution Gap between "it's probably fine" and a documented posture.

Deploy the walled / zero-retention config + auto-refresh

We stand up the right mode (ZDR API, self-hosted, or VPC-walled) and re-check vendor terms and sub-processor lists on a schedule (Upstash · Vercel · Exa), alerting you when a retention term or sub-processor changes, so your posture never silently goes stale.
