The Operator Execution Gap · live

Ask a generic AI "what's happening in my market." Watch it fail.

Large language models answer market questions most confidently exactly where their training data is stalest. Same question, two answers: the fluent-but-outdated generic reply, and the primary-source, dated one. The distance between them is what a wrong assumption costs.

✕ Generic LLM · unsourced & stale

"Give me a competitive summary of my category — who's winning and what should I do?"

"Your category is stable and growing steadily. Your main competitor is the market leader and is focused on enterprise. Pricing is flat across the space, and there's no major new entrant to worry about."

Confident. Fluent. Frozen on training data of unknown age — and blind to the funding round, the pricing change, and the hiring surge that happened last quarter. The exact way a strategy deck ends up wrong in the room.

What actually moves the picture

Six signal streams, by decision consequence

The signals that move real money and real positioning, and the public source each is read from. Generic summaries collapse all six into "the market is fine."

Competitor moves High impact

Product launches, feature shifts, positioning changes, press. Read from newsrooms, changelogs, and captured pages: dated, not summarized.

Pricing & packaging High impact

Timestamped captures of public pricing pages surface increases, new tiers, and quiet removals before your win-rate tells you.

Customer sentiment Moderate

Review platforms and public forums, read for recurring complaints and shifts: an interpretive signal, flagged as such.

Hiring signals Moderate

Public job postings reveal where a competitor is investing (a GTM surge, a new market, an R&D bet) weeks before the launch.

Funding & filings High impact

SEC EDGAR (Form D, 10-K, 8-K) and public round announcements: the highest-confidence signal of a competitor's next runway.

IP & regulatory Nuance

Patent & trademark filings (USPTO) and relevant regulatory actions hint at direction: directional, confidence-scored.

How it works

A repeatable intelligence method, not a one-off scrape

Every Aloha AI monitor runs the same three-stage pipeline. It's why the read stays right next month, not just today.

Retrieval Intelligence Layer (RIL)

Primary and verifiable public sources only (SEC filings, public job postings, captured pricing pages, review platforms, patent & trademark filings), captured with source and retrieval date. No headline aggregation.

Resolve to an Operator Layer (ROL)

Raw signals become a decision a leader can act on: what changed, for whom, with what confidence, closing the Operator Execution Gap.

Auto-refresh & alert

The monitor re-checks its primary sources on a schedule (Upstash · Vercel · Exa) and can ping you when a competitor files, prices, or hires, so it never goes stale.
