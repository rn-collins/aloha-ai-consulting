✓ Aloha AI · primary-source grounded FDA · CSA

Same question, answered from the record:

No. MDMA-assisted therapy is not FDA-approved. In August 2024 the FDA issued a Complete Response Letter to Lykos Therapeutics declining approval for PTSD; the FDA published the CRL on Sep 4, 2025. As of July 2026, Lykos is running an additional Phase 3 trial and preparing a resubmission. No approval exists.

MDMA remains a Schedule I controlled substance federally, meaning no accepted medical use and no legal prescribing pathway. There is no state where MDMA-assisted therapy is a licensed, above-ground service. Legal, above-ground psychedelic therapy today means regulated psilocybin (Oregon, Colorado), not MDMA.

What actually governs

Three substances, three completely different regimes

The single biggest source of confusion in this space is treating "psychedelics" as one legal category. They are not. Each moves on its own track.

Psilocybin State-regulated, Sch I federally

Schedule I under the federal CSA. But Oregon (Measure 109 / ORS 475A, services live since 2023) and Colorado (Prop 122 / SB23-290, first session Jun 2025) run state-licensed supervised-use programs. Not a prescription model; facilitated administration at licensed centers.

MDMA-assisted therapy Not approved

Schedule I. The FDA declined approval (CRL, Aug 2024; published Sep 2025). No above-ground legal service anywhere in the U.S. Resubmission pending; watch the additional Phase 3 trial and any FDA advisory-committee action.

Ketamine Sch III · legal, prescribable

Schedule III and FDA-approved (as an anesthetic; esketamine/Spravato for depression). Legally prescribable today. The live risk is how it's prescribed: telehealth without a prior in-person exam runs on a temporary DEA flexibility.

The federal / state gap Nuance

"Legal in my state" never means "legal federally." Oregon and Colorado programs operate despite federal Schedule I status: an enforcement-discretion posture, not federal legalization. Map each activity to both layers.
