The Operator Execution Gap · live

Ask a generic AI what to do. Watch it miss the mission.

The default pitch to a cultural institution is "add an AI chatbot to your site." It sounds efficient and gets the sector exactly wrong, because it ignores standards, provenance risk, and access obligations. Same brief, two reads. The distance between them is what a wrong build costs a collection and an audience.

✕ Generic vendor · "add a chatbot"

"We want AI for our museum — where do we start?"

"Add an AI chatbot to your website so visitors can ask questions, and use AI to auto-write labels and auto-tag your whole collection. Quick win, launch in a week."

Fluent, cheap, demo-ready. Wrong for a cultural institution on three counts — it fabricates attributions, breaks your metadata standards, and can fail the accessibility rule you're now legally bound to.

✓ Aloha AI · mission-grounded read CIDOC-CRM · WCAG · AAM

Same brief, answered from the sector's standards:

Start where the risk and the payoff are highest, not where the demo is easiest. An AI cataloguing assist should map to CIDOC-CRM / Dublin Core fields and resolve terms against the Getty AAT / ULAN, with a human registrar approving every attribution. Nothing is auto-published.

A public "chatbot" only helps if it is grounded in your own catalogue (no invented provenance) and meets WCAG 2.1 AA, the standard DOJ requires of large public entities' web content and kiosks as of April 24, 2026. Provenance triage must follow AAM/AAMD due-diligence, not an LLM's guess.

Confidence: High  Why you can rely on it: it maps to published, verifiable sector standards, cited in the evidence table below with retrieval dates.

OEG (the Operator Execution Gap) is the distance between what generic AI says and what a cultural institution can responsibly act on. Closing it is the entire job.

How it works

A repeatable method, not a one-off model drop

Every Aloha AI build runs the same three-stage pipeline. It's why the concept holds up to a registrar, a grant panel, and an accessibility auditor, not just a demo.

Reference Intelligence Layer (RIL)

Ground the build in real, published sector standards (CIDOC-CRM, Dublin Core, Getty vocabularies, WCAG, AAM/AAMD provenance guidelines) captured with source and retrieval date. No hallucinated attributions, no invented provenance.

Resolve to an Operator Layer (ROL)

Standards become a build a curator or ED can act on: what the AI does, what stays under human approval, what changes for your audience, closing the Operator Execution Gap. Informed by neuro-aesthetics: how attention, trust, and memory actually form in a gallery.

Ground, refresh & re-verify

The build re-checks its grounding sources on a schedule (Upstash · Vercel · Exa) and flags when a standard revises (e.g. a WCAG update or a provenance-portal change) so it never quietly goes stale.
