The Operator Execution Gap · live

Ask a generic AI the one question that matters. Watch it fail.

Large language models fail most confidently exactly where an operator's stakes are highest. Same question, two answers: the fluent-but-wrong generic reply, and the primary-source-grounded one. The distance between them is what a wrong assumption costs.

✕ Generic LLM · unsourced

"Is marijuana Schedule III now — are state dispensaries federally legal?"

"Yes — marijuana was rescheduled to Schedule III, so cannabis is now federally recognized as medicine and state-legal dispensaries are in the clear."

Confident. Fluent. Wrong on three counts — and wrong in the exact way that gets a tax position, a funder memo, or a filing rejected.

✓ Aloha AI · primary-source grounded CFR · Fed. Reg.

Same question, answered from the record:

Only a partial move has taken effect. As of April 28, 2026, a DEA final order moved two narrow categories to Schedule III: marijuana within an FDA-approved drug product, and marijuana under a state medical-marijuana license. Broad rescheduling is still contested in an ALJ hearing (Jun 29–Jul 15, 2026) where all seven designated participants oppose it.

Schedule III does not make state adult-use dispensaries federally legal: those substances still require a prescription and DEA registration, which state markets don't satisfy. What it does change: §280E taxes, research access, and FDA-approved products.

Confidence: High  Why you can rely on it: it maps to the operative Federal Register order and the live docket, cited below, with retrieval dates.

OEG, the Operator Execution Gap, is the distance between what AI says and what a regulated operator can act on. Closing it is the entire job.

What actually changes

Schedule I → Schedule III, by operator consequence

The four shifts that move real money and real risk. The April 28 order already applied the first to FDA-approved products and state-licensed medical marijuana.

§280E federal tax High impact

Schedule I/II (now)
§280E denies ordinary deductions; effective rates often exceed 70%.
Schedule III
§280E no longer applies. Deductions return, the single largest cash impact.

Production quotas High impact

Schedule I/II (now)
DEA aggregate production quotas apply (21 CFR §1303.11).
Schedule III
Outside the APQ regime. Supply planning changes materially.

Research access Moderate

Schedule I/II (now)
Schedule I registration + protocol review, a years-long barrier.
Schedule III
Lower registration burden; clinical research eased.

Adult-use market Nuance

Common assumption
"Schedule III legalizes dispensaries."
Reality
No. Sch III still needs a prescription + DEA registration; state adult-use stays non-compliant.
