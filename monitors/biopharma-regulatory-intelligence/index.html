The Operator Execution Gap · live

Ask a generic AI the one question that matters. Watch it fail.

Large language models fail most confidently exactly where a drug program's stakes are highest: pathway and exclusivity facts. Same question, two answers. The fluent-but-wrong generic reply, and the primary-source-grounded one. The distance between them is what a wrong assumption costs your runway.

✕ Generic LLM · unsourced

"My small-molecule NCE gets orphan designation — how many years of market protection is that, and does designation itself block generics?"

"Orphan designation gives you 7 years of exclusivity that starts the day FDA designates the drug, and it blocks all generics for the whole condition — so you're protected from filing onward."

Confident. Fluent. Wrong on three counts — and wrong in the exact way that mis-sequences a launch, a raise, or a partnering term sheet.

✓ Aloha AI · primary-source grounded 21 CFR · FDCA

Same question, answered from the record:

Designation is not exclusivity. Orphan-drug exclusivity is 7 years but runs from the date of marketing approval, not from designation (21 CFR §316.31). It protects only the approved indication or use — not the whole rare disease — and can be broken if the holder can't assure supply.

Separately, a small-molecule new active moiety may earn 5-year NCE data exclusivity under Hatch-Waxman (a generic may file a Paragraph IV ANDA at year 4). Orphan and NCE exclusivity are distinct clocks that can overlap. Plan to both.

Confidence: High  Why you can rely on it: it maps to 21 CFR Part 316, the FDCA exclusivity provisions, and FDA guidance, cited below with retrieval dates.
