✓ Aloha AI · platform-policy grounded Policy category

Same post, diagnosed against the actual policy surface:

The suppression is a claims problem, not a schedule problem. Phrases that imply a direct physiological effect, such as "boosts metabolism," "balances hormones," or "detoxifies," fall under platforms' health-and-wellness / unproven-claims category, which restricts distribution and ad eligibility regardless of how many studies you attach.

The EDSA fix: keep the Evidence, reframe the claim as discussion rather than promise, respect the reader's self-determination ("here's what the research explores" not "this will fix you"), and give a compliant action. Same rigor, inside the policy line.

Confidence: High  Why you can rely on it: it maps to published platform health-and-wellness policy categories and the LegitScript ad-eligibility gate, described in the method ledger below, with review dates.

OEG (the Operator Execution Gap) is the distance between what AI says caused a problem and what actually did. Naming the real mechanism is the entire job.

What actually suppresses reach

Four throttles, and the EDSA move that clears each

These are policy categories platforms publish and enforce, not folklore. Each maps to one part of the EDSA structure: Evidence · Discussion · Self-determination · Action.

Unproven / prohibited health claims High risk

ThrottleCure/diagnosis/direct-effect language ("balances hormones," "cures anxiety") triggers the health-and-wellness restriction: distribution and ad eligibility both drop.
EDSA · DiscussionReframe the promise as inquiry: "what the research explores," not "what this does to you."

Ad-eligibility gate (certification) High risk

ThrottleMany health, pharmacy, telehealth and addiction-treatment advertisers must be LegitScript-certified to run ads on Google, Meta and others. Uncertified = disapproved.
EDSA · EvidenceEstablish verifiable legitimacy (licensing, certification path) so paid distribution is even available.

Off-platform links & sourcing Moderate

ThrottleOutbound links and unvetted product/source URLs are commonly deprioritized in organic feeds; risky domains can flag the whole post.
EDSA · ActionGive a compliant next step natively; move commercial links to a low-risk destination the platform trusts.

Sensitive-audience targeting Nuance

ThrottleBody-image and health-condition targeting is restricted (e.g., minor exclusions, placement limits). Configuration, not content, suppresses reach here.
EDSA · Self-determinationSpeak to the reader as decision-maker; drop exploitative framing that trips sensitive-category rules.

How it works

A repeatable audit method, not a one-off reach hack

Every Aloha AI audit runs the same three-stage pipeline, so the diagnosis holds up against the next policy update, not just the current feed.

Regulatory Intelligence Layer (RIL)

Read the actual policy surfaces: published platform content and ad standards, health-and-wellness categories, and certification requirements, captured with source and review date. No engagement folklore.

Resolve to an Operator Layer (ROL) via EDSA

Raw policy becomes a content decision you can act on: which claim to reframe, which link to move, what to certify. Structured as Evidence · Discussion · Self-determination · Action, it closes the Operator Execution Gap.

Auto-refresh & alert

Platform policies change often. The audit re-checks its policy sources on a schedule (Upstash · Vercel · Exa) and can ping you when a standard shifts, so your content system doesn't drift back into suppression.
