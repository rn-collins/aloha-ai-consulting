What actually shapes trust

Four levers that move perception, and the order they fire in

Brand trust is not one thing. It is a stack of cognitive mechanisms, each with its own lever and its own failure mode when you skip it.

Processing fluency Foundational

The brain treats easy-to-process as safe and true. Simpler marks, higher contrast, one clear claim: read faster, judged more favorably. This is the floor; nothing above works until it's in place.

Mere exposure Compounding

Repetition converts fluency into liking, largely below awareness. Consistency is not a style preference. Each identical encounter is processed a fraction faster, and that ease is felt as preference.

Source credibility Conditional

Specific, verifiable proof and named expertise raise trust, but only once you are already fluent and familiar. Loud claims from an unfamiliar source are effortful, and effort is discounted.

Affect heuristic Fast & first

A quick "good/bad" feeling forms before deliberation and biases everything after it. Aesthetic ease pre-loads that feeling, which is why craft is a trust input, not decoration.
