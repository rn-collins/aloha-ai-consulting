The problem

Two ways legal AI goes wrong

Since 2023, courts have sanctioned lawyers for filing AI-fabricated citations. The reaction splits into two failures, and both cost you.

Banned, so people use it in the shadows

A blanket prohibition doesn't stop AI use; it drives it off the record, onto personal accounts, with no verification and no audit trail. The exposure moves out of sight, not out of existence.

Trusted blindly, so a fabrication ships

Speed without a verification gate is how a hallucinated case name reaches a filing. The Operator Execution Gap, the distance between what AI says and what you can put your bar standing behind, is exactly what has to be closed.

The method

Where verification sits in the workflow

Trust isn't a setting you toggle on. It's a set of gates placed where risk actually lives, so the fast steps stay fast and the load-bearing steps get checked.

Layer 01 · Retrieval

Ground the answer in a real source

Route research through authorities the model can actually cite, so the starting point is a document that exists, not a plausible-sounding memory.

Layer 02 · Citation check

Verify every citation against the record

Each case, statute, and pin cite is confirmed to exist and to say what the draft claims it says, before a human ever reads it as settled.

Layer 03 · Human gate

A named reviewer signs off on the load-bearing steps

Supervision is designed into the flow, so responsibility is explicit and the record shows who checked what, in line with professional-conduct duties.

Layer 04 · Audit trail

Leave a record leadership can defend

What the model produced, what was verified, and who approved it, captured so the firm can show its work if anyone asks.
