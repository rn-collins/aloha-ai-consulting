Framework: Neuroscience-of-Trust Content Architecture (EDSA)

The gap most regulated creators never see

The moment you publish a video about psilocybin, cannabis, or a prescription drug, two things appraise it. The first is a human nervous system, running a fast pre-deliberative check: expert or amateur, honest or selling, safe or a threat. If that appraisal returns "threat," nothing you say afterward lands.

The second is a classifier. A recommendation system runs its own threat appraisal: safe to circulate, or near a policy line. If it returns "threat," your video is quietly withheld from the feed. Not removed, just never shown. No audience. No appeal. No notification.

Most regulated creators optimize for one judge and lose to the other. Content the brain distrusts does not convert. Content the classifier reads as a threat does not reach anyone. You can be compliant and invisible, or compelling and suppressed. The trap is thinking those are your only two options.
