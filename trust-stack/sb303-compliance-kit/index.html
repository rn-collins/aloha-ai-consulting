Framework: Audit-Ready Operations

What a missed window costs

Your license does not come off the wall because a session went wrong. It comes off the wall because a number was late. Every quarter, Oregon Psilocybin Services opens a fixed reporting window, and when it closes it does not reopen. Confirm the current window and its close time against OPS before you build your calendar.

Miss it and you have created a compliance event, on the record, that OPS may treat as grounds for an administrative penalty. Submit numbers that do not hold up and you put false-statement and misrepresentation exposure on the table under the division-333 rules.

The failure is almost never the reporting itself. It is upstream: a client counted in the wrong quarter, an opt-out client included in totals, an average dose computed without the secondary doses, a copy stored off-premises. None feels like a violation in the moment. All of them are, and they surface when a regulator is already looking.

What's inside

  • The Audit-Ready Operations framework. Compliance reframed as engineered trust with the regulator, with the three rules every section is built to satisfy.
  • A cited requirements map. Every SB 303 data duty, split into collecting, storing, reporting, and sharing, each pinned to its exact ORS/OAR/OPS source in plain language.
  • A live-pull method for the reportable fields. How to pull the exact current service-center data OPS collects each quarter, and any computed averages, straight from the current OPS form, so it never goes stale.

  • An equity-data strategy and a compliance checklist, plus a Verification Ledger marking every requirement verified or flagged for you to confirm.
