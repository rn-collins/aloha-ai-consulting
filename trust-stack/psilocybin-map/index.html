What being wrong in front of a funder costs

You are across the table from a program officer, a state licensing analyst, or an investor's counsel. You make a claim about what New Mexico's law requires. They already know the answer. If your number matches theirs, the rest of your pitch gets heard. If it does not, the conversation is over.

The meeting you spent months getting, gone the instant your fact is checkable and wrong. The grant you were positioned for, reassigned to the applicant whose numbers held up. The client engagement, because an operator siting a service center cannot act on "roughly."

The errors are not cosmetic. New Mexico's binding statutory implementation deadline is 31 December 2027, not the 2026 date printed on the agency's own page. Colorado's facilitator dollar fees are not published in any rule, so every figure circulating for them is unsourced. An intelligence product that does not catch those errors has become them.

What's inside
